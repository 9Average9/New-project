Fighter:

You have four superiority dice, which are d8s. A superiority die is expended when you use it. You regain all of your expended superiority dice when you finish a short or long rest. You gain another superiority die at 7th level and one more at 15th level.

Take an additional action on your turn.

Heal yourself by using a bonus action. (2-d4s)

Make additional attacks during your Attack action.

Add superiority dice to your attack rolls.

On your turn, you can use a bonus action and expend one superiority die to bolster the resolve of one of your companions. When you do so, choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you. That creature gains temporary hit points equal to the superiority die roll + your Charisma modifier.

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to frighten the target. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, it is frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

Reroll a failed saving throw.

Eldritch Knights, a Fighter archetype, can cast spells from the wizard spell list. These can range from offensive spells like Fireball to utility spells like Mage Armor.

A Fighter can use this maneuver to attempt to disarm an opponent, forcing them to make a Strength saving throw or drop an item they're holding.

When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and a superiority die to make a melee weapon attack against that creature if it's within your reach.

Fighters can use their reaction to add their proficiency bonus to their AC when they're hit by an attack, potentially turning a hit into a miss.

Monk:

Monks can make an unarmed strike as part of the Attack action. At 1st level, this strike deals 1d4 damage plus the Monk's Dexterity modifier. This damage increases to 1d6 at 5th level, 1d8 at 11th level, and 1d10 at 17th level.

Spending 1 ki point, a Monk can make two additional unarmed strikes as a bonus action after taking the Attack action. The damage for each strike is based on the Monk's unarmed strike progression.

For 1 ki point as a bonus action, a Monk can take the Dodge action until the start of their next turn, giving attackers disadvantage on attack rolls against the Monk.

By spending 1 ki point, a Monk can use the Dash or Disengage action as a bonus action, along with doubling their jump distance for the turn.

Monks can use their reaction and spend 1 ki point to reduce damage from a ranged weapon attack they can see by 1d10 + Dexterity modifier + Monk level. The Monk's level is also added to the damage they can throw back if they reduce the damage to 0.

When a Monk hits with a melee attack, they can spend 1 ki point to attempt to stun the target. The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of the Monk's next turn.

Monks of the Way of Shadow can spend 2 ki points to cast the Pass Without Trace spell and teleport between shadows as a bonus action.

Monks of the Way of the Open Hand can use 1 ki point to Disengage or Dash as a bonus action, doubling their jump distance for the turn.

At 18th level, a Monk can spend 4 ki points to become invisible for 1 minute, gaining resistance to all damage except force and becoming immune to all conditions.

By spending 2 ki points, a Monk can heal themselves for an amount equal to three times their Monk level.

At 17th level, Monks can spend 3 ki points and use an action to attempt to deliver an attack that could potentially kill a creature, as described previously. The damage inflicted remains constant, but the Monk's level affects their ability to attempt the lethal strike.

(Level 1) A cleric releases a shimmering projectile towards a target within range, making a ranged spell attack. On a hit, the target takes 4d6 radiant damage. Higher-level casting allows increased damage; for each slot level above 1st, the damage increases by 1d6.

(Level 1)A cleric can heal an ally within range for 1d4 + spellcasting ability modifier hit points. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the healing; for each slot level above 1st, the healing increases by 1d4.

(Level 1) The cleric touches a creature, healing it for 1d8 + spellcasting ability modifier hit points. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the healing; for each slot level above 1st, the healing increases by 1d8.

(Level 2) This spell creates a floating spectral weapon within 60 feet of the cleric. As a bonus action, the caster can make a melee spell attack using their spellcasting ability modifier, dealing force damage on a hit. Using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher increases the damage; for each slot level above 2nd, the damage increases by 1d8.

(Level 3) Clerics can heal multiple allies within a 60-foot range for 1d4 + spellcasting ability modifier hit points. Using a spell slot of 4th level or higher increases the healing; for each slot level above 3rd, the healing increases by 1d4.

(Varies) At 8th level, some Clerics gain the ability to infuse their weapon strikes with divine energy. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage.

(Level 2) At 2nd level, a cleric can use Channel Divinity to heal allies. The cleric can restore up to 5 times their cleric level in hit points. Higher-level clerics can heal for more hit points as they advance in levels.

(Level 1) A cleric can grant a creature within range a +2 bonus to AC for the duration. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the duration; for each slot level above 1st, the duration increases by 10 minutes.

(Level 1) A cleric blesses up to three creatures within range, granting them a +1d4 bonus to attack rolls and saving throws. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the number of creatures affected; for each slot level above 1st, one additional creature can be affected.

(Level 1) Make a melee spell attack against a creature you can reach. On a hit, the target takes 3d10 necrotic damage. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the damage; for each slot level above 1st, the damage increases by 1d10.




Barbarian:

A Barbarian can enter a rage as a bonus action, granting them advantages in combat. While raging, they gain bonus damage on melee weapon attacks, resistance to certain types of damage, and advantage on Strength checks and saving throws.

Barbarians can choose to attack recklessly, gaining advantage on melee weapon attack rolls during their turn but granting advantage to enemies attacking them until the start of their next turn.

Barbarians excel in close combat, making attacks with melee weapons. They add their Strength modifier to the damage dealt with melee attacks.

At 3rd level, some Barbarians can choose the Path of the Berserker and gain the Frenzy ability. This allows them to make an additional attack as a bonus action on each of their turns while raging.

At 5th level, Barbarians can attack twice whenever they take the Attack action on their turn, instead of once.

Starting at 9th level, Barbarians roll an additional damage die when scoring a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, increasing to two additional dice at 13th level and three additional dice at 17th level.

At 11th level, if a Barbarian's rage ends early due to a drop to 0 hit points, they can make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw to retain 1 hit point and remain conscious.




Paladin:

(Level 5) A Paladin's weapon attacks deal an extra 5d10 force damage on a hit, and if the target has 50 hit points or fewer after taking this damage, it must succeed on a Charisma saving throw or be banished. Using a spell slot of 6th level or higher increases the force damage; for each slot level above 5th, the force damage increases by 1d10.

(Varies) When a Paladin hits with a melee weapon attack, they can expend a spell slot to deal radiant damage to the target in addition to the weapon's damage. The damage scales with the spell slot's level, adding 1d8 radiant damage for a 1st-level spell slot and an additional 1d8 for each higher-level slot.

(Varies) Paladins can heal wounds by touching a creature and spending points from their pool. At 10th level, they can heal up to 50 hit points per long rest using this feature.

(Level 1) Paladins can bless up to three creatures within range, granting them a +1d4 bonus to attack rolls and saving throws. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the number of creatures affected; for each slot level above 1st, one additional creature can be affected.

(Level 1) A Paladin's weapon attacks deal extra psychic damage on a hit and force the target to make a Wisdom saving throw or become frightened. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the damage; for each slot level above 1st, the damage increases by 1d6.

(Level 1) A Paladin can grant a creature within range a +2 bonus to AC for the duration. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the duration; for each slot level above 1st, the duration increases by 10 minutes.

(Level 1) Paladins can imbue their weapon with radiant energy, granting them additional damage on their weapon attacks. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the damage; for each slot level above 1st, the damage increases by 1d4.

(Level 1) A Paladin's weapon attacks deal extra thunder damage on a hit, and the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed 10 feet away and knocked prone. Using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher increases the damage; for each slot level above 1st, the damage increases by 2d6.

(Level 1) At 7th level, a Paladin can use their Channel Divinity to gain resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from weapon attacks for 1 minute.

(Level 11) Paladins of the Oath of Vengeance can use their reaction to make a melee weapon attack against a creature that damaged them when they are not incapacitated.

(Level 1) Paladins of the Oath of Vengeance can use a bonus action to choose a creature within 10 feet and gain advantage on attack rolls against that creature for 1 minute or until it drops to 0 hit points or falls unconscious.




Rogue:

A Rogue can deal extra damage when they have advantage on an attack roll or when an ally is within 5 feet of the target. The damage increases with Rogue levels, starting at 5d6 at level 10 and increasing to 6d6 at level 13, 7d6 at level 16, and 8d6 at level 19.

Rogues can make an additional attack as a bonus action using a light melee weapon held in their off hand.

Rogues can take a bonus action to Dash, Disengage, or Hide in combat, enhancing their mobility and positioning for attacks.

At level 7, Rogues gain the ability to evade the brunt of area effects, taking half damage on a failed Dexterity saving throw and no damage on a successful save.

Starting at level 5, when an attacker the Rogue can see hits them with an attack, they can use their reaction to halve the attack's damage.

At level 17, Rogues gain the ability to take advantage on attack rolls against creatures that haven't taken a turn in combat and any hit they score against a surprised creature is a critical hit.

Rogues of the Shadow subclass gain the ability to teleport from one shadow to another, gaining advantage on their next attack if they attack immediately afterward.

At level 20, Rogues can turn any miss into a hit once per turn or turn any failed ability check into a success, allowing for precise and successful attacks even on missed rolls.

At level 17, when a Rogue hits a creature that is surprised and is within 5 feet, they can maximize the damage of their Sneak Attack against that creature.

Rogues are proficient in making melee attacks with finesse weapons, adding their Dexterity modifier to the attack and damage rolls.

Rogues can use ranged weapons like a shortbow or crossbow to make precise attacks from a distance.




Ranger:

Rangers are skilled in making ranged attacks with weapons like bows or crossbows, utilizing their Dexterity modifier for attack and damage rolls.

Rangers can make an additional attack as a bonus action using a light melee weapon held in their off hand.

Rangers can mark a target as their quarry, dealing an extra 1d6 damage whenever they hit it with a weapon attack. Casting this spell at higher levels increases the damage; for each spell slot level above 1st, the extra damage increases by 1d6.

Hunters gain the ability to deal extra damage to wounded targets. Once on each of their turns, when they hit a creature with a weapon attack, they can deal an extra 1d8 damage if it's below its hit point maximum.

At level 15, Rangers gain the ability to make opportunity attacks when creatures leave their reach without using their reaction, giving them additional attacking opportunities.

Hunters at level 11 gain the ability to make a melee attack against any number of creatures within 5 feet of them, provided they have taken the Attack action on their turn.

Rangers of the Gloom Stalker subclass gain the ability to add their Wisdom modifier to their initiative rolls and make an additional attack on the first turn of combat.

Rangers with companions like the Beast Master's animal companion can command their companion to make attacks, providing an additional source of damage in combat.

At level 7, Rangers gain the ability to evade the brunt of area effects, taking half damage on a failed Dexterity saving throw and no damage on a successful save.

At level 20, Rangers gain the ability to add their Wisdom modifier to the attack and damage rolls against their favored enemies.

Hunters at level 11 can make a ranged attack against any number of creatures within 10 feet of a point they can see, adding their Dexterity modifier to the attack roll.

At level 10, Rangers can spend a minute creating camouflage to effectively hide even when observed if they remain stationary.
